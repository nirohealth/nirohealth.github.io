"I Drive 10 Hours a Day. Sciatica Almost Made Me Park My Car For Good."

By Naveen R. · Bangalore · Last Updated: Feb 18, 2026

Let me tell you something about driving for a living.

People think it's easy. "Bus baithke steering ghoomana hai." Sit and turn the wheel. But after 10-12 hours in that seat — hitting every pothole on Outer Ring Road, braking in Silk Board traffic, your spine absorbing every vibration — your body pays a price nobody sees.

I've been driving for Ola in Bangalore for 6 years. Before that, I drove a Tavera for a tour company in Mysore. I'm 40 years old and I've spent the better part of my adult life sitting in a driver's seat.

And 2 years ago, my body finally said enough.

It didn't start as sciatica. It started as "driver's back pain."

Every driver I know has back pain. It comes with the job. You learn to live with it. A little stiffness in the morning, some ache after a long day — you stretch a bit, lie down, and it's manageable.

But what started happening to me was different.

The pain in my lower back started travelling. First into my left hip. Then down the back of my thigh. Then — and this was the part that scared me — a burning, electric feeling that went from my buttock all the way to my calf.

Like someone was running a hot wire through my leg.

"Bhai, pair sun ho jaata tha ride ke baad. Kabhi kabhi brake pe pair rakhta tha aur lagta tha pair hai hi nahi. Tab darr laga."

— What I told my wife when the numbness started

After a ride, my left leg would go numb. Sometimes while driving — my foot on the brake would lose feeling. That's when the fear hit me. This wasn't just back pain anymore. This was dangerous.

I went to a government hospital. X-ray. Then MRI. The doctor said: "L4-L5 disc bulge. Sciatic nerve compression. You need physiotherapy and rest."

Rest. As if a cab driver earning daily can take "rest."

The math that keeps you driving through pain

Here's what people with salaried jobs don't understand. If I don't drive, I don't earn. Period.

₹20,500 per month. That's not just money — that's my daughter's tuition, my wife's household budget, my EMI.

So I kept driving. Through the pain. Through the numbness. Popping Aceclofenac tablets on an empty stomach. Rubbing Volini on my back in the car between rides.

Until even that stopped working.

Everything I tried — and why none of it lasted

  • Volini / Moov / Iodex — 10-15 minutes of cooling sensation. Pain came right back. Wasted money buying one tube after another. At one point I was going through 3 tubes a month.
  • Painkillers (Aceclofenac, Diclofenac) — Worked for 4-5 hours, then the pain returned worse. After 2 months, developed severe acidity. Doctor said stop immediately or risk stomach ulcer.
  • Ayurvedic oil from chemist — Horrible smell. Stained my car seat. Passengers complained. One lady gave me a 1-star rating saying "driver smells like medicine." Relief lasted 20 minutes. Useless.
  • Physiotherapy — ₹600 per session, twice a week. I went for 6 weeks. Genuine improvement during the sessions, but I couldn't maintain consistency. If I missed even one week, the pain came crashing back.
  • Belt / back support — Bought a lumbar support belt from Amazon. It helped the posture a bit but did nothing for the shooting leg pain. Sciatica is not a posture problem — it's a nerve problem.

Total spent in 2 years: roughly ₹50,000-60,000.

That's 3 months of my daughter's school fees. Gone. On things that gave me hours of relief at best.
